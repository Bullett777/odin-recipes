Heaven's Tomahawk Steak

Description

This wagyu tomahawk recipe is so awesome. It is a show stopper but most important delicious. It takes a little work, but if you put the time to work on this steak, I guarantee you that anyone who tries this will thank you forever. It might just be the best recipe of 2019!

Ingredients

  • 2 Tomahawk ribeye ( I prefer Wagyu A3 or better)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Garlic Powder
  • 4 cups Beef Broth
  • 1 Stick of Butter (8 tbsp)
  • All Purpose Flower
  • 2 Egg Yolks
  • Catupiry cheese
  • Mexican Crema
  • Salt n Papper to Taste
  • Finish with Chives to Taste

Steps

    The Beef

  1. Clean and trin the meat (you'll want to make sure they are about 1-1/2" thick
  2. Season with salt, pepper, and garlic powder till evenly coated. (This is a dry brine) let it sit for at least 2 hours.
  3. You'll want to cook them to an internal temp of 120 degrees.
  4. Once to temp, take it off the grill and chop it up. (save the bone)
  5. You will then add the chopped pieces of steak and catupiry cheese into a large mixing bowl and combine.
  6. In the bowl add chives, salt and black pepper to the meat and chees mixture, combione thoroughly.
  7. Next you'll want to roll out some saran wrap. Add about half of the mixture in a small rounded area and stick the bone straight up in the middle.
  8. Roll the mixture around the bone until in a ball, the classic coxinha look. Repeat for the other one.
  9. After wrapping it, tie the mixture where the saran wrap meets the bone with butcher's twine.
  10. Trim the excess saran wrap off, then sit them each in a separate rounded bowl about the same size as the bone and meat mixture. (that way they stay rounded)
  11. Stand them upright with the bone pointed up, and freeze them for 1 to 2 hours.

    The Dough

  1. In a large sauce pan over medium high heat add; beef broth, 1 stick of butter, creme de leite and combine until smooth.
  2. Season this mixture with salt and black pepper to taste.
  3. Next add your flour, there is no exact amount just until you have a nice consistency.(add slow and little by little)
  4. Your dough should not be sticking to the pan and it should not be dry.
  5. Once its combined and at the right consistency(playdoughish),. add the 2 egg yolks and combine thoroughly.(this should make a more smooth texture)
  6. On some saran wrap lay the dough and flatten it out. (divide the dough inhalf) Then take the tomahawk mixture out of the freezer and cut the saran wrap off it.
  7. next sit the tomahawk mixture in the middle of once of the flatten pieces of dough. Wrap the dough around and up the bone. (just like with the saran wrap)
  8. Trim any excess dough off.
  9. Then wrap the dough and meat mixture again with saran wrap( just like the first time), round and sit in the same bowls.
  10. Freeze them again for 1 hour.
  11. Next its time to bread them!(1 flour, 2 egg-wash, 3 breadcrumbs)
  12. once breaded sit them in the bowls again.

    Making it tasty

  1. Deep fry the whole thing at 350 degrees in corn oil.(use a basket to sit them in)
  2. once fried, cook them on indirect heat until the center is done.
  3. ENJOY!
